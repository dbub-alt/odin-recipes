Kimchi-Jjigae

Description

I learned to make this after buying kimchi on a whim and having no idea what to do with it. This dish is awesome. It's a little out of the ordinary, but it's hearty, hot, and delicious. It's a fantastic one pan dish that everyone will love for dinner. Serve with rice to cut the heat.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb kimchi, cut into bite size pieces
  • 1/4 cup kimchi brine
  • 1/2 lb pork belly, cubed
  • 1/2 - 2/3 package of tofu, sliced
  • 3 green onions, 2 sliced diagonally and 1 sliced thinly
  • 1 white onion, diced
  • 8 oz shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp gochugaru (Korean hot pepper flakes)
  • 1 tbsp gochugang (hot pepper paste)
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 cups anchovy stock

Anchovy Stock Ingredients

  • 7 large dried anchovies, head and guts removed
  • 1/3 cup Korean or daikon radish, sliced thinly
  • 4x5 in kombu (dried kelp)
  • green onion roots
  • 4 cups water

Steps

  1. Make your anchovy stock
    1. Add your anchovies, radish, green onion roots, and kombu in a sauce pan.
    2. Add the water
    3. Bring to a boil over medium high heat and leave boiling for 20 minutes.
    4. Lower the heat to low for 5 minutes.
    5. Strain
  2. Brown your pork belly over medium high heat.
  3. Add the kimchi and the kimchi brine.
  4. Add the white onion.
  5. Add the green onions.
  6. Add the salt, sugar, gochugaru, and gochujang.
  7. Drizzle the sesame oil over top.
  8. Add the anchovy stock.
  9. Cover and cook for 10 minutes over medium high heat.
  10. Open and mix. Lay the tofu over top and ladle some broth over it.
  11. Cover and cook another 10-15 minutes over medium heat.
  12. Add the thinly sliced green onions.
  13. Eat it. Love it. Write me a thank you letter.
